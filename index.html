I create things for the web.

I started creating things for the web when I was in middle school and realized everyone has the tools to make professional websites—they just need to learn how to use them. So I started teaching myself HTML and CSS, building my own web sites, and uploading them to the Internet.

When I worked as a clerk at a health food store, I offered to ramp up the store's web presence to help it compete in the digital age. By the time I left Healthy Alternatives, I was managing its social media pages as well as writing and producing a monthly newsletter. I increased newsletter subscriptions by 15% without decreasing engagement. I also developed a new, mobile-friendly site for the store.

In addition to using the Internet as my playground, I'm also passionate about making the world a better place. In fact, I got a degree in professional writing to learn how I could use my voice to help those less fortunate than me. I've put those skills to use with The Humane League, Raging Chicken Press, and more throughout the years.

If you need someone who thrives on building things behind the scenes and has the organization skills to handle big projects, I'd love to chat.
